Weapon Mechanics

In Brawlhalla, weapon management plays a crucial role in combat. Learn how to pick up, attack, throw, and even lose your weapon strategically!

Picking Up

Weapons appear as flaming swords of light at semi-random intervals in specific areas of the map. When a Legend interacts with them, they transform into usable weapons.

Spawn mechanics:

  • 1v1 Mode: The first weapon pickup spawns at the center of the map.
  • 2v2 Mode: A pickup spawns on each side of the map.
  • Other Modes: Pickups appear randomly within designated spawn areas.

Weapon pickups alternate, ensuring that each new pickup is different from the last one collected.

Weapon lifespan:

  • Untouched pickups remain on stage for a limited time.
  • Weapons flash for 2.5 seconds before disappearing.
  • Weapons vanish after 30 seconds, while most Gadgets disappear within 15 seconds.

Using the Sidekick Summoner allows a Legend to summon a weapon pickup directly to their location, providing a tactical advantage.

Attacking

Each weapon in Brawlhalla has a unique moveset, allowing for diverse playstyles and strategies. Attacks are divided into different categories:

  • Ground Light Attacks (GLA): Fast and versatile attacks used for initiating combos.
  • Aerial Light Attacks (ALA): Quick airborne strikes that help control space and extend combos.
  • Ground Heavy Attacks (GHA): Powerful moves that deal significant damage and knockback.
  • Aerial Heavy Attacks (AHA): Strong airborne attacks used for finishing opponents.

Each Legend has Signature Moves (Sigs), which are unique heavy attacks tied to their weapons. These moves define a Legend’s playstyle and provide distinct advantages.

Additionally, weapons have different attack properties:

  • True Combos: Guaranteed sequences of attacks that cannot be escaped.
  • Strings: Flexible attack chains that allow for creative follow-ups.
  • Active Input: Some weapons, like Scythe and Battle Boots, allow directional control over certain attacks.

Mastering attack timing, spacing, and movement is crucial for dominating battles. Each weapon has strengths and weaknesses, making adaptability key to success.

Throwing

Weapons can be thrown in combat using the Throw action, similar to Gadgets. Each weapon has a unique hitbox size, and throws can be charged to increase speed, knockback, and damage.

Most weapons have the same throw speed, except:

  • Hammer: 0.85x normal speed
  • Spear: 1.1x normal speed

Thrown weapons bounce off surfaces but stop dealing damage after losing enough velocity—usually within two bounces.

Once a weapon lands on stage (stops spinning), it begins disappearing:

  • Can be picked up again by the thrower for 11 frames
  • Disappears completely after 10 additional frames

Thrown weapons cannot be picked up by another Legend, even if they share the same weapon.

In case of a throw clash, a thrown weapon passes through enemies if the thrower was stunned within the last 25 frames.

Losing your weapon

Legends can lose their weapon when they take enough damage or are hit by a strong attack. The disarm threshold is determined by:

  • Total damage taken: The more damage a Legend accumulates while holding a weapon, the higher the chance of disarming.
  • Incoming attack force: Stronger attacks increase the likelihood of losing the weapon, especially when a Legend’s health reaches red.

Once disarmed, a Legend must pick up another weapon or gadget to reset their damage threshold.

Additional mechanics:

  • Weapon retention: If a Legend throws their weapon before being hit, they avoid disarming.
  • Weapon denial: Opponents can control weapon spawns to limit a disarmed Legend’s options.
  • Recovery strategies: Using unarmed attacks effectively can help regain control until a new weapon is picked up.

Mastering disarming mechanics allows for strategic plays, whether forcing opponents to lose their weapon or recovering quickly after being disarmed.
